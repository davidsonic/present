Experimental results for IROS

Experiments completed

  1. User study of 25 users, 5 subjects for each object
  2. 40 models tested (25 from user), 5 pretrained, 5 self-supervised, 5 joint-trained with simulated adversary
  3. Randomized challenging environment

Results

  1. Self-adversary > Self-supervised > pretrained
  2. Human-adversary (chosen) > Self-adversary
  3. Human-adversary (average) <= Self-adversary

By-products

  1. First interactive grasping environment with perturbation input
  2. Show that grasping policy can learn with 0-1 weakly supervised reward
  3. Show that adversary can facilitate protagonist policy training
  4. Distribution of human adversary differs from simulated adversary
  5. Real world user data collected
