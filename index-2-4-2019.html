Progress on adversarial grasping

Created by Jiali Duan on Feb.04, 2019

Modifications of protagonist reward

Motivation: explore inherent task structure to accelerate convergence
  1. Reaching: in [0,1], to encourage reaching object
  2. Grasping: in [0,0.25], non-zero if arm is grasping object
  3. Lifting: in [0,1], non-zero if arm has lifted the object

Modifications of adversary pipeline

Motivation: more reasonable adversary training
  1. Duration: after lifting and lasts until the end of episode
  2. Perturbation success: check contact force to determine if adversarial network needs update
  3. Determine loss: weak supervision without groundtruth

Train adversarial policy

Motivation: Need to be fast, because of human adversary training
  1. Classification vs regression
  2. Transfer learning vs from scratch
  3. Discretized vs continuous

Pipeline Overview

  1. System reset and data caching
  2. Inverse kinematic control
  3. Loss collection and training

System reset and data caching

  1. Init scene with top-down camera + 3D->2D conversion
  2. Init protagonist network and cache its session
  3. Init adversary network and cache its session

Inverse Kinematic Control

  1. Detect (x,y,angle) with protagonist network + 2D->3D conversion
  2. Init scene with free camera + cartesian coordinate-> joint coordinate conversion
  3. Perform inverse kinematic control

Training

  1. Collect reward for protagonist policy (3 parts: reaching/grasping/lifting)
  2. Predict perturbation (6x1) with adversary network/apply human adversary + collect adversary loss
  3. Backpropagate reward and loss
