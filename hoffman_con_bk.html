Cons of "Cost-Based Anticipatory Action Selection for Human-Robot Fluency"

Presented by Jiali Duan on Jan.29th, 2020

Paper Information

  • Author: Guy Hoffman and Cynthia Breazeal (2007)
  • Motivation: Stop-to-go -> Anticipatory action selection
  • Contribution
    1. A model for human-robot fluent-action meshing
    2. Three fluency metric hypotheses
    3. Analysis for Reactive Agent vs Anticipatory Agent

Outline

  • Cons in terms of Problem Definition
  • Cons in terms of Model
  • Cons in terms of Analysis
  • Future Work
  • Q & A

Cons in terms of Problem Definition


  1. Invovling only two agents (one robot and a human), with a shared workbench
    2. Reason:
    • Multi-agent scenario?
    • Multiple workbenches?

Cons in terms of Model


  1. Assumption
    • stationary human-collaborator behavior
    • Expected cost of proto-sequence requires $h<2d_{0i}$, where h is not accessible
    • Expectation calculation assumes performing actions correctly (deterministic)

Cons in terms of Analysis


  1. $h$ matters and is fixed during evaluation
  2. Solution:
    • h should be specific per state
    • Be learned over time as the agent collects more data regarding the turnaround time of the human teammate

Cons in terms of Analysis


  1. Defects in three metric hypothesis
    • A simplifying assumption that the start and end time of the whole task is identical for both agents
    • The team is made up of one human and one robot
    • Do not cover certain aspects of cooperative motion and physically coupled collaboration

Future Work

  1. Add a discount factor in the learned state transition distribution -> recent moves more salient
  2. h should be state-specific
  3. Evaluate the relative effect achieved by the state transition distribution learning
  4. Increase task complexity
